Following the national studio meeting and LEAN workshop, my focus was on what the architecture / design profession sells, what sets it apart from other professions and professionals, defining this and considering a possibility of what an AOOTF could be based on this…

  1. What do we sell?
    We sell our imagination; we practice creativity
    We sell our skill to realise our (or a client’s) imagination by means of producing and monitoring a set of instructions
  2. Definitions of what we sell

    Imagination (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • The faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses
    • The ability of the mind to be creative or resourceful
    • The part of the mind that imagines things

    Creativity (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • The use of imagination or original ideas to create something; Inventiveness

    Skill(definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • The ability to do something well; expertise
    • A particular ability

    Produce (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • Make or manufacture from components or raw materials
    • Make (something) using creative skills

    Realise (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • Cause to happen
    • Achieve (something desired or anticipated); fulfil
    • Give actual or physical form to

    Monitor (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • A person who observes a process or activity to check that it is carried out fairly or correctly, especially in an official capacity

    Instructions (definition, Oxford Dictionary):

    • Detailed information about how something should be done or operated
  3. Feeding the creative soul is important. There is a magic in creating something. It gives our profession purpose. Having somewhere to express, test and learn from our imagination is vital to realise and develop ideas and practice creativity.
  4. LaboratoryWorkshopPlayroomSchoolGalleryLibraryStudio (not office): A space to nurture the creative soul and imagination
    • It’s a workshop, because we build physical things and ideas and hone our skills
    • It’s a playroom, because we have fun
    • It’s a laboratory, because we produce useable objects
    • It’s a school, because we learn things
    • It’s a gallery, because we display and critique what we have built and produced
    • It’s a studio, because we do the above in an artful way
  5. Leave it to technology to document our imagination
    We could be modelling with real stuff with software that documents instructions from which to build.


    Just as the example above relies on large block (Lego Duplo), a system is being developed for regular Lego pieces such as Lego Architecture studio

    What if, similar to how pixel technology has developed over the past 15 years and where it’s heading,


    Lego blocks are replaced with kinetic sand or plasticine?

  7. Scope – what does this look like?
    Just as a construction site is our office, our AOOTF could not resemble a traditional office as we know it. For example, if you don’t count the builder’s site office, there are no desks on a construction site.

    Do we need to reflect a corporate conservatism at all? Why? Our profession is inherently based on creativity. Is it a less orchestrated space? Can we have a meeting on the floor or leaning against a wall?
    The AOOTF could enable ourselves to go to work to investigate, create, play, perfect, learn and admire every day when you feel the need to.
  8. How could this AOOTF operate?
    Foster spontaneity with ‘bump’ / chance meetings with other colleagues to expand the richness and quality of our collective imagination. Nurture ‘core’ purpose. At all hours?

Does it fall under the banner of BVN, not necessarily under the BVN brand? Can it be trialled within the current studio as a secondment?
Do we hold classes and invite external / community participation and learning?
